Leadership
The ability to influence, motivate, and enable others to contribute toward organizational success.
Management
The process of planning, organizing, leading, and controlling resources to achieve specific goals.
Stakeholder
Any individual or group affected by or with an interest in a business's operations, such as employees, customers, or investors.
Entrepreneur
A person who creates, organizes, and runs a business, taking on financial risks in the hope of profit.
Strategy
A long-term plan designed to achieve specific business objectives.
SWOT Analysis
A tool used to identify a business’s internal Strengths and Weaknesses, and external Opportunities and Threats.
Organizational Structure
The way in which a business arranges its people and tasks to meet its goals.
Delegation
The process of assigning responsibility and authority to others to complete tasks.
Motivation
The internal or external drive that encourages individuals to take action and achieve goals.
Productivity
The measure of output per unit of input, such as labor hours.
Marketing
The activities a business undertakes to promote the buying or selling of products or services.
Branding
The process of creating a unique name, image, or identity for a product in consumers’ minds.
Budget
A financial plan that outlines expected income and expenses over a certain period.
Cash Flow
The movement of money in and out of a business, affecting its liquidity.
Profit Margin
A financial metric that shows what percentage of revenue is profit after costs are deducted.
Human Resources (HR)
The department responsible for hiring, training, and managing employees within a company.
Delegation
The assignment of responsibility or authority to another person to carry out specific tasks.
KPI (Key Performance Indicator)
A measurable value that indicates how effectively a company is achieving its objectives.
Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR)
A business’s commitment to act ethically and contribute to economic development while improving quality of life for employees, the local community, and society.
Innovation
The process of creating new ideas, products, or methods that add value to a business.
