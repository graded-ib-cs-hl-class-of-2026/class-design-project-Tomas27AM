Supply
The total amount of a good or service that producers are willing and able to sell at a given price.
Demand
The quantity of a good or service that consumers are willing and able to buy at various prices.
Opportunity Cost
The value of the next best alternative that is forgone when making a decision.
Scarcity
The fundamental economic problem of having limited resources to meet unlimited wants.
Inflation
The general increase in prices across an economy over a period of time.
GDP (Gross Domestic Product)
The total value of all goods and services produced within a country in a given year.
Market
Any structure that allows buyers and sellers to exchange goods, services, or resources.
Equilibrium
The point at which the quantity supplied equals the quantity demanded in a market.
Capital
Man-made resources (like tools, machinery, and buildings) used to produce goods and services.
Labor
The human effort, both physical and mental, used in the production of goods and services.
Microeconomics
The branch of economics that studies individual consumers, firms, and markets.
Macroeconomics
The branch of economics focused on the economy as a whole, including issues like inflation, unemployment, and economic growth.
Monopoly
A market structure where a single seller dominates the entire market with no competition.
Competition
The rivalry among businesses to sell their goods and services to consumers.
Elasticity
A measure of how much the quantity demanded or supplied of a good responds to a change in price.
Fiscal Policy
Government decisions about spending and taxation to influence the economy.
Monetary Policy
The process by which a central bank controls the money supply and interest rates to influence the economy.
Recession
A period of economic decline typically identified by a fall in GDP for two consecutive quarters.
Trade-Off
A situation in which gaining one thing requires losing another due to limited resources.
Subsidy
A government payment that supports a business or market to lower the cost of production or encourage certain behavior.
